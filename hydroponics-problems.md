# Common Problems & Engineering Solutions

## Biofilm Formation

Biofilm is a layer of bacteria, organic residue, and minerals inside pipes.

Effects:

- reduces pipe diameter
- decreases water flow
- clogs emitters

Biofilm can develop rapidly in nutrient-rich environments.

---

## Emitter Clogging

Common causes:

- bacterial growth
- mineral precipitation
- organic debris

Small irrigation emitters are particularly vulnerable.

---

## Algae Growth

If light reaches water reservoirs:

- algae form quickly
- oxygen levels drop
- pH fluctuates

Prevention:

- opaque reservoirs
- closed tubing
- light shielding

---

## Microbial Contamination

Hydroponic nutrient solutions create ideal environments for microorganisms.

Risks:

- plant diseases
- root rot
- contamination buildup

---

## Root Blockage

In systems like NFT:

- roots grow into channels
- water flow becomes blocked

This can completely stop irrigation.

---

## System Bottlenecks

Major engineering bottlenecks:

- pipe clogging
- bacterial biofilm
- algae growth
- root blockage
- uneven water distribution

Design strategies:

- large channels instead of narrow tubes
- minimal tubing
- easy disassembly for cleaning

---

## Summary

Hydroponic systems face challenges primarily related to:

- water behavior
- biological growth
- root development
- hydraulic design

The hardest problems are typically:

- biofilm
- clogging
- algae
- root blockage
- uneven water flow

Engineering solutions focus on:

- simple plumbing
- large flow channels
- easy cleaning
- stable water circulation
